# **1. Game Overview**

## **1.1 Title**

Centipede (Reverse Engineered)

## **1.2 Genre**

* Arcade Shooter
* Fixed Screen Shooter

## **1.3 Platform**

* Web (HTML5 Canvas / JS)
* Desktop (optional wrapper)

## **1.4 Core Concept**

The player controls a shooter at the bottom of the screen and must eliminate a descending segmented centipede while avoiding and destroying other enemies. The battlefield evolves dynamically through mushrooms that alter movement patterns and difficulty.

---

# **2. Core Gameplay Loop**

1. Player moves horizontally (and limited vertical within player area)
2. Player fires upward
3. Centipede moves horizontally, descending when blocked
4. Player destroys segments → creates mushrooms
5. New enemies spawn (Spider, Flea, Scorpion)
6. Player survives waves → difficulty increases
7. Repeat until death / score max

---

# **3. Controls**

## **3.1 Input**

* Arrow Keys / WASD → Movement
* Spacebar → Fire

## **3.2 Movement Constraints**

* Player restricted to bottom ~20% of screen
* Cannot move into UI or top playfield

---

# **4. Game World**

## **4.1 Coordinate System**

* Grid-based logical system
* Continuous rendering (pixel-based)

### Suggested Grid:

* Width: 30 columns
* Height: 30 rows
* Cell size: ~16x16 px

---

# **5. Level & Map Design**

## **5.1 Playfield Zones**

### **Top Zone (Enemy Spawn Zone)**

* Rows: 0–10
* Centipede begins here
* Fleas spawn if mushroom density low

### **Middle Zone (Combat Zone)**

* Rows: 10–20
* Mushrooms accumulate heavily
* Centipede fragmentation increases

### **Player Zone (Safe Zone)**

* Rows: 20–30
* Player restricted here
* Spider operates primarily here

---

## **5.2 Mushroom System**

### Properties:

* Health: 4 hits
* Grid anchored
* Alters centipede path

### Effects:

* Causes centipede to descend
* Creates maze-like patterns

### Spawn Rules:

* Initial: Random distribution (~30–40 mushrooms)
* Dynamic: Created when centipede segments destroyed

---

## **5.3 Map Evolution**

Over time:

* Mushroom density increases
* Movement becomes more chaotic
* Player visibility decreases
* Difficulty scales organically

---

# **6. Player Mechanics**

## **6.1 Movement**

* Speed: constant
* Pixel-based smooth movement
* Collision with mushrooms prevents overlap

## **6.2 Shooting**

* Single bullet on screen (classic mode)
* Straight vertical trajectory
* Bullet speed faster than enemies

---

# **7. Enemy Systems**

---

## **7.1 Centipede**

### Structure:

* Head + body segments
* Each segment = independent entity after split

### Movement Rules:

1. Move horizontally (left/right)
2. On:

   * Screen edge OR
   * Mushroom collision
     → Move down 1 row
     → Reverse direction

### Behavior:

* Head leads
* Segments follow previous segment path

### Splitting:

* When segment destroyed:

  * Chain splits into two centipedes
  * New head forms at split

---

## **7.2 Spider**

### Zone:

* Player area (bottom)

### Movement:

* Erratic zig-zag
* Diagonal patterns
* Variable speed

### Behavior:

* Hunts player
* Destroys mushrooms

---

## **7.3 Flea**

### Spawn Condition:

* Mushroom count below threshold (~5)

### Behavior:

* Drops vertically from top
* Leaves mushrooms behind randomly

---

## **7.4 Scorpion**

### Movement:

* Horizontal across screen

### Effect:

* Poisons mushrooms

### Poison Effect:

* Centipede ignores descent rules
* Moves straight downward rapidly

---

# **8. Collision System**

## **8.1 Bullet Collisions**

* Bullet vs Enemy → destroy enemy
* Bullet vs Mushroom → damage mushroom

## **8.2 Player Collisions**

* Player vs Enemy → death
* Player vs poisoned centipede → instant death
