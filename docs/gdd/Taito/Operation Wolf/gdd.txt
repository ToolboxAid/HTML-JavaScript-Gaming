# OPERATION WOLF — REVERSE ENGINEERING GAME DESIGN DOCUMENT

---

# 1. Game Identity

**Title:** Operation Wolf
**Developer/Publisher:** Taito
**Release:** 1987 (Arcade)
**Genre:** Rail shooter / light gun shooter
**Core Concept:**
The player uses a mounted **Uzi-style light gun** to fight through enemy waves, rescue hostages, and survive a sequence of scripted combat zones.

---

# 2. High Concept

A **fixed-path (rail) shooter** where:

* The camera auto-scrolls through combat zones
* The player aims and fires freely on screen
* Enemies appear in waves from multiple directions
* Resource management (ammo, grenades, health) is critical

---

# 3. What Makes Operation Wolf Unique

* Physical **Uzi gun controller** (recoil feedback feel)
* Blend of:

  * shooting skill
  * target prioritization
  * resource management
* Hostage protection system (don’t shoot civilians)
* Semi-realistic military tone vs arcade abstraction

---

# 4. Player Fantasy

The player is:

* A lone commando behind enemy lines
* Managing chaos under pressure
* Making rapid decisions:

  * shoot vs conserve ammo
  * grenade vs save
  * risk vs survival

---

# 5. Controls

## 5.1 Arcade Controls

* **Light Gun (Uzi-style):**

  * Aim: point-and-shoot
  * Trigger: fire bullets
* **Grenade Button**
* **Start / Coin**

## 5.2 Feel Requirements

* Instant hit feedback
* High firing rate
* Slight recoil illusion (visual + audio)
* Smooth tracking across screen

---

# 6. Camera & Movement

## 6.1 Rail System

* Player does **not move character**
* Screen scrolls horizontally through level
* Player controls only:

  * Aim
  * Shooting

## 6.2 Zones

Each level consists of **scripted encounter segments**:

* Jungle
* Camp
* Vehicles
* Boss zones

---

# 7. Core Gameplay Loop

1. Screen scrolls into combat area
2. Enemies spawn from:

   * ground
   * buildings
   * vehicles
3. Player shoots targets
4. Collect pickups
5. Protect hostages
6. Survive until zone ends
7. Continue to next area

---

# 8. Weapons & Resources

## 8.1 Primary Weapon (Uzi)

* Automatic fire
* Limited ammo
* Reload required when empty

## 8.2 Grenades

* Area-of-effect attack
* Limited count
* Used for:

  * vehicles
  * groups
  * bosses

---

# 9. Resource Systems

## 9.1 Ammo

* Depletes with firing
* Reload animation/lockout
* Can be refilled via pickups

## 9.2 Grenades

* Limited inventory
* Strategic usage required

## 9.3 Health

* Player takes damage from:

  * bullets
  * explosions
* Health packs restore life

---

# 10. Target Types

## 10.1 Enemy Soldiers

* Primary targets
* Appear from cover, run, shoot
* Different speeds and behaviors

## 10.2 Vehicles

* Trucks, tanks, boats
* Require sustained fire or grenades

## 10.3 Helicopters

* Boss or mini-boss units
* High durability
* Moving targets

## 10.4 Hostages (CRITICAL)

* Must NOT be shot
* Shooting them:

  * penalizes player
  * may reduce score or affect progression

---

# 11. Pickup System

## 11.1 Types

* Ammo refill
* Grenades
* Health packs

## 11.2 Behavior

* Dropped by enemies or scripted spawns
* Must be shot to collect (classic arcade mechanic)

---

# 12. Enemy AI Behavior

## 12.1 Spawn Patterns

* Scripted by level timeline
* Appear from:

  * sides
  * doors/windows
  * vehicles

## 12.2 Actions

* Shoot at player
* Move across screen
* Take cover

## 12.3 Target Priority Design

Players must decide quickly:

* Immediate threats (shooting enemies)
* High-value targets (vehicles)
* Resource drops
* Avoiding hostages

---

# 13. Damage System

## 13.1 Player Damage

* Hit by enemy bullets → health decreases
* Explosions → higher damage

## 13.2 Enemy Damage

* Bullet hits accumulate damage
* Some enemies die instantly
* Vehicles require sustained damage

---

# 14. Level Structure

## 14.1 Stage Flow

Each stage includes:

1. Entry segment
2. Combat waves
3. Vehicle encounters
4. Boss fight

## 14.2 Boss Examples

* Attack helicopter
* Heavy armored units

---

# 15. Difficulty Model

Difficulty scales through:

* Faster enemies
* More enemies simultaneously
* Reduced reaction windows
* More incoming fire
* Tougher bosses
* Resource scarcity

---

# 16. Scoring System

## 16.1 Score Sources

* Killing enemies
* Destroying vehicles
* Completing stages
* Accuracy (implicit)

## 16.2 Penalties

* Shooting hostages
* Wasting ammo (indirectly)

---

# 17. Game Feel Requirements (CRITICAL)

To feel like Operation Wolf:

* Shooting must feel **fast and responsive**
* Enemies must create **constant pressure**
* Player must feel **overwhelmed but capable**
* Audio feedback (gunfire) must be strong
* Hit feedback must be immediate and clear

---

* type

* attack pattern

## 18.4 LevelScript (CRITICAL)

* timeline-based event system
* defines:

  * spawn times

  * boss triggers
  * pickup drops

---

# 19. Collision System

* Raycast from gun → hit detection
* Hitboxes for:

  * enemies
  * pickups
  * hostages

Priority:

1. Player shot → enemy
2. Player shot → hostage (penalty)
3. Player shot → pickup (collect)
4. Enemy shot → player

---

# 20. Core Algorithms

## 20.1 Hit Detection

* Instant raycast from cursor
* No projectile travel time (arcade style)

## 20.2 Spawn System

* Timeline-driven (not random)
* Predefined encounter sequences

## 20.3 Target Selection Pressure

* Multiple simultaneous threats
* Forces prioritization

---

# 21. Audio-Visual Identity

## 21.1 Visuals

* Military theme
* Jungle/war environments
* Explosions, muzzle flashes

## 21.2 Audio

* Rapid gunfire (signature sound)
* Explosions
* Enemy voices

* Strong tactile + audio feedback loop

**Operation Wolf is controlled chaos.**

It succeeds because:

* Every shot matters (ammo is limited)
* Every decision matters (targets vs hostages)
* The game flows like a cinematic war sequence
