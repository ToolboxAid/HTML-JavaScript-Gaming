The arcade landscape of the 1980s and 1990s was defined by a handful of legendary manufacturers like Atari,
Namco,
Nintendo,
Capcom,
and Sega. While thousands of titles were released,
the "Top 100" typically features the industry-shaping classics that dominated the Golden Age (80s) and the fighting/3D revolution (90s). [1]

## Era Highlights

* The 1980s (Golden Age): Dominated by single-screen puzzle-action games ([Pac-Man](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/0jt_x&q=top+100+arcade+games+of+the+80s-90s,+and+who+made+them)) and intense shooters ([Galaga](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/02xmmk&q=top+100+arcade+games+of+the+80s-90s,+and+who+made+them),
[Defender](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/02k4l6&q=top+100+arcade+games+of+the+80s-90s,+and+who+made+them)). Manufacturers like Atari and Namco were the primary leaders during this period.
* The 1990s (Technical Evolution): Shifted toward multiplayer "beat 'em ups" (TMNT),
the fighting game phenomenon started by Capcom's [Street Fighter II](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/01j8j2&q=top+100+arcade+games+of+the+80s-90s,+and+who+made+them),
and the birth of 3D racing and fighting by Sega and Namco. [1,
4]
