A few things are still worth locking down before build.

The biggest missing piece is a **tie-break rule** for who owns the top when two pingwins are effectively equal height. Without that, scoring can feel inconsistent. Good choices are center proximity, last uncontested owner, or highest world Y plus velocity fallback.

You also still need a precise **damage model**. Right now HP exists, but the GDD does not yet say how much damage comes from collisions, abilities, stomps, falling, or environmental hazards.

The **water / recovery platform rules** could use one more pass. You have “fall into ocean or recover on a platform,” but not yet:

* how many platforms can exist
* whether platforms move
* whether enemies or abilities can hit players in water
* whether players are invulnerable while swimming

The **co-op / multiplayer session rule** should be clarified a little more. You have single-player and co-op, but it would help to say whether the win condition is always individual, or whether there is ever a team-based variant.

The **iceberg wrap rule** is good, but it should define whether projectiles, power-ups, and enemies also wrap, or only players.

The **ability targeting system** could use final input rules:

* mouse aim, right stick aim, or directional-only
* whether aiming slows movement
* whether throw arcs are fixed or charge-based

The **power-up carry rule** should be explicit:
gravity should play in for special ability
* one carried at a time
* auto-activate or manual activate
* dropped on death or lost on death (barrial at sea would be very kewl.)

The **break event** is strong, but it still needs exact behavior:

* is the break purely visual + collision shape change
* or does a chunk fully separate and drift
* does the “top” relocate instantly or transition over time

The **leaderboard model** should define whether “fastest win” is tracked:

* per character
* per map
* globally
* local machine only or network profile later

The **match configuration defaults** should be pinned down:

* default win time
* default HP
* default respawn delay
* default power-up spawn frequency
* default break trigger at 65 percent is set, but timer length is not

And one very practical item: you still want a short **controls section** in the GDD. Even if temporary, it helps build faster:

* move
* jump
* ability
* power-up
* pause
* character select confirm

So, no major concept is missing. The game idea is solid. What’s left is mostly **numbers, tie-breaks, input specifics, and edge-case rules**. The most important three to add before build are:

1. top ownership tie-break
2. damage/HP rules
3. controls/input map
